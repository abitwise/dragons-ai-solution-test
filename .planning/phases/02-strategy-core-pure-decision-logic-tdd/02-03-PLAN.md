<objective>
Drive the shop decision test-first (STRAT-04 heal + STRAT-05 upgrade): decide whether to buy a healing potion (`hpot`, looked up by id in the live shop list) when lives are low and gold allows, and — only when lives are healthy — whether to buy the priciest affordable non-`hpot` upgrade while reserving a 100-gold healing buffer. Costs are read LIVE from the passed-in `ShopItem[]`, never hardcoded.

Purpose: Survival is the scoring lever — the game ends at `lives === 0`, so longevity = more attempts = more score (D-08). Heal-before-upgrade ordering (D-09) and the reserved buffer (D-10) prevent the bot from spending the gold that keeps it alive (PITFALLS #4 heal-before-risk; FEATURES competitor analysis: keep >=2 potions in reserve). Reading costs from the live shop honors the explicit "no hardcoded shop ids/costs" out-of-scope rule (REQUIREMENTS Out of Scope) since the catalog can change.

Output: shop-decision function(s) in `src/strategy.ts` (naming at executor discretion per CONTEXT — one function returning the chosen `ShopItem | null`, or a heal fn + an upgrade fn), plus `MAX_LIVES_TO_KEEP` and `HEAL_BUFFER_GOLD` constants; tests in `src/strategy.test.ts` covering heal-triggers / not-affordable / upgrade-with-buffer / priciest-affordable / nothing-to-buy.
</objective>
